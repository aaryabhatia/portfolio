Smart India Hackathon 2024

CAD Model Designer Team Forge

As part of Team Forge, developed a compact floating waste collection system for lakes and rivers — taking on full mechanical design ownership and delivering a complete CAD model and system layout within a 4-hour timeframe.

Overview

As part of the Smart India Hackathon, our team developed a concept for removing floating waste from water bodies such as lakes and rivers. The idea was to create a compact system capable of continuously collecting and storing debris.

My role focused entirely on mechanical design, where I developed the complete system layout and CAD model within a 4-hour timeframe, prioritising functionality, stability, and real-world feasibility.

Design Process

The system was designed using a catamaran (twin-float) configuration to ensure stability and balanced weight distribution. A front intake geometry combined with angled side panels passively guides floating debris toward a central conveyor pathway.

The conveyor transfers waste into a rear-mounted modular storage unit, allowing quick removal and continuous operation. The layout was optimised to maintain a compact footprint while maximising effective collection width, ensuring efficiency without compromising manoeuvrability.

Features

The design maintains stability through a twin-float architecture with controlled mass distribution to reduce pitch under varying loads. The intake geometry enables passive debris guidance, improving efficiency without increasing system size, while the centrally aligned conveyor ensures smooth load transfer and minimal structural imbalance.

The structure is lightweight and manufacturable, with provisions for 3D-printed (PETG) components and modular assembly. A detachable storage unit allows quick waste handling and continuous operation.

An IoT-based control layer complements the system, integrating camera input, user commands, and proximity-based waste level sensing within a central processing unit, with cloud connectivity enabling remote monitoring.
