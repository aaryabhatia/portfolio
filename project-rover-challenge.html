Overview

As the Design and Simulations Head for Team Kosmos during SPROS IRC 2024, I worked on designing key mechanical systems for the rover within a strict two-month timeline.

My responsibilities included designing a robotic arm with an end effector, contributing to the development of a rocker bogie chassis, and creating custom housings and smaller components for electronics.

The existing rover design had two major issues: the double wishbone suspension exceeded the competition's dimensional limits, and the previous robotic arm was bulky and heavy.

Key Contributions

  • Designed a robotic arm with a Factor of Safety (FOS) of 3.69.
  • Helped develop a rocker bogie chassis from scratch.
  • Designed and 3D printed an ABS end effector.
  • Created custom mounts, brackets, and electronics housings.
  • Assisted in redesigns and troubleshooting manufacturing failures.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Completed design, validation, and manufacturing within two months.
  • Had limited time for 3D printing and iterative prototyping.
  • Repaired a broken and jammed end effector during competition using acetone welding, Araldite, and reinforced metal sheets.
  • Reinforced a bent robotic arm after a vendor supplied weaker aluminum by welding a thicker aluminum block onto the structure.
