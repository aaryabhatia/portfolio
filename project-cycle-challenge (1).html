Bharath Cycle Design Challenge 2023

Finalist Team Nova

As part of Team Nova, reached the national finals of the AICTE-hosted Bharat Cycle Design Challenge 2023 with an innovative rear-steering tricycle concept designed for short-distance urban mobility — combining the functions of a tricycle, stroller, and utility carrier into a single vehicle platform.

Overview

As part of Team Nova, I participated in the Bharat Cycle Design Challenge 2023, hosted by AICTE, under the Non-EV Commute Cycle category. The competition focused on developing innovative mobility solutions across four segments: EV Commute, EV Commercial, Non-EV Commute, and Non-EV Commercial cycles.

Our concept aimed to solve a practical urban mobility problem for parents, especially mothers travelling short distances with young children. The final design combined the functions of a tricycle, stroller, and utility carrier into a single vehicle platform.

Design Process

The project began with concept generation and market research to identify user needs in the non-EV mobility segment. After exploring multiple ideas, we finalised a child-friendly transportation concept focused on stability, comfort, and utility.

The entire vehicle was designed in Fusion 360, including the frame, steering layout, and basket structure. Special attention was given to balance, load distribution, and rider ergonomics.

The competition received over 800 applications nationwide, and our design was selected as one of four finalists in the Non-EV Personal Cycle category, among sixteen finalists overall. AICTE provided ₹40,000 to support prototype fabrication for the final exhibition round.

Design Innovation and Features

Our concept was a rear-steering tricycle designed to make short-distance mobility easier for parents, especially mothers travelling with young children.

  • Three-wheel layout with two wheels at the front and one at the rear, combined with a rear steering mechanism for improved manoeuvrability and stability.
  • Front-mounted basket designed to safely carry babies or young children, with cushioning and a protective shade for comfort.
  • Basket repurposable for carrying goods or storage items, adding everyday utility beyond child transport.
  • Frame engineered for a payload capacity of over 80 kg, with dual headlights and a tail light for visibility.
  • Flexible configuration supporting use as either a single-seater or two-seater vehicle.
